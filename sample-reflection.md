# Sample Reflection for Testing Analysis Schemas

## Relationship Reflection - Week of March 15th

### The Incident

Last Tuesday, I had what I thought would be a simple conversation with my partner about our upcoming vacation plans. It turned into something much more complex and emotionally draining.

I approached them while they were watching TV and said, "Hey, I was thinking we could look at booking our flights for the trip to Italy. The prices seem to be going up, and I want to make sure we get good seats."

Their immediate response was to roll their eyes and say, "You're always so controlling about money. Why can't you just relax for once?" This caught me off guard because I was genuinely trying to be helpful and organized.

I felt my chest tighten and my heart start racing. I tried to stay calm and said, "I'm not trying to control anything. I just thought it would be good to plan ahead." But they interrupted me and said, "You always do this. You make everything about money and schedules. It's exhausting."

### The Escalation

I could feel myself getting defensive, but I tried to keep my voice steady. "I don't think that's fair. I was just suggesting we look at options."

That's when they stood up and said, "You know what? I don't want to go on vacation with someone who's going to micromanage everything. Maybe you should just go alone." They walked out of the room, leaving me standing there feeling completely blindsided.

I followed them into the kitchen and said, "Wait, can we talk about this? I don't understand why you're so upset." They turned around and said, "Because you never listen to me. You always think you know what's best, and you don't care about what I want."

### The Aftermath

I felt like I was walking on eggshells for the rest of the evening. They wouldn't look at me, and when I tried to apologize, they said, "Don't bother. Your apologies are always empty."

Later that night, I heard them on the phone with their friend, and I could hear them saying things like, "She's so controlling, it's ridiculous" and "I don't know how much longer I can deal with this." It felt like a betrayal.

The next day, they acted like nothing happened. They were sweet and affectionate, asking me what I wanted for dinner and offering to watch a movie together. But I couldn't shake the feeling that something was wrong.

### My Emotional State

I've been feeling anxious and confused ever since. I keep replaying the conversation in my head, wondering what I did wrong. I've been having trouble sleeping, and I find myself second-guessing everything I say around them.

I feel like I'm constantly trying to prove that I'm not controlling or selfish, but nothing I do seems to be enough. I've started avoiding bringing up any topics that might be "controversial" because I'm afraid of another outburst.

### The Pattern

This isn't the first time something like this has happened. It seems like whenever I try to be proactive or organized about something, it gets twisted into me being "controlling" or "selfish."

Last month, when I suggested we create a budget for our household expenses, they accused me of not trusting them with money. When I tried to plan a date night, they said I was being "too structured" and "not spontaneous enough."

I'm starting to feel like I can't do anything right. I'm walking on eggshells constantly, and I'm afraid to express my needs or opinions because I know they'll be turned against me.

### What I Need

I need to feel heard and respected in this relationship. I need to be able to express my thoughts and concerns without being attacked or dismissed. I need my partner to take responsibility for their reactions and communicate with me in a healthy way.

I want to be able to plan things together without being accused of being controlling. I want to feel like my input matters and that my partner values my perspective.

### Questions I Have

- Am I really being controlling, or is this a pattern of gaslighting?
- How can I communicate my needs without triggering these reactions?
- Is this relationship healthy for me?
- What boundaries do I need to set to protect my mental health?

### Additional Context

We've been together for two years, and this pattern has been getting worse over the past six months. I've noticed that they seem to be more critical of me lately, and I feel like I'm constantly trying to prove my worth in the relationship.

I've been considering therapy, both individual and couples, but I'm worried about bringing it up because I know they'll see it as another way I'm trying to "control" or "fix" things.

I love them, but I'm starting to question whether this relationship is good for me. I feel like I'm losing myself trying to accommodate their needs while mine are constantly dismissed or criticized.
